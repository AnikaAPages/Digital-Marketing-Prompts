HubSpot & Email Marketing

5 prompts
01 HubSpot
HubSpot Email Performance Audit
Here are the HubSpot performance metrics from my last [5 / 10] email sends: [paste: send date, subject line, list size, Clicks %, Click-Through Rate %, unsubscribes, bounces]. Identify patterns across what's working and what's underperforming. Flag any deliverability concerns based on bounce or unsubscribe trends. Give me 3 specific changes to test in my next send based on the data.
02 HubSpot
Subject Line Generator
Write 10 email subject lines for [brand/publication] promoting [offer / sponsored content / editorial / event] to [audience segment]. Vary the approach across: curiosity, urgency, benefit-led, conversational, and direct. Flag your top 3 picks for A/B testing in HubSpot and explain why each would likely perform. Keep all lines under 50 characters.
03 Email
Sponsored Email Writer
Write a sponsored email for [publication] on behalf of [brand partner] promoting [offer / event / product / content] to [subscriber audience]. Include: subject line, preview text, opening hook, 2–3 sentences of body copy that feel editorial not salesy, a clear CTA button label, and an optional P.S. line. Tone: [warm / premium / direct].
04 Email
Nurture Sequence Builder
Build a 4-email nurture sequence in HubSpot for [brand] targeting [new subscriber / lead / past customer]. Each email should have a clear, distinct purpose: welcome and set expectations, educate or add value, build trust with proof, then convert. For each: subject line, preview text, and 2–3 sentence body copy direction. Note the recommended send delay between each email.
05 Email
Re-Engagement Campaign
Write a 2-email re-engagement sequence for [brand] in HubSpot targeting contacts who haven't opened in [60 / 90 / 180] days. Email 1: a warm, low-pressure "we miss you" with a clear reason to re-engage. Email 2: a final send with a compelling value offer and an honest message about what happens if they don't engage. Keep both under 120 words and flag recommended HubSpot suppression steps after the sequence.

GA4 & Website Analytics

5 prompts
06 GA4
GA4 Website Performance Interpreter
Here is our GA4 data for [date range]: [paste metrics] — including Engagement Rate, Sessions, Total Users, Views, Pages Per Session, and % of Returning Visitors. Interpret what this data is actually telling me about how our audience is using the site. Flag anything that needs attention and suggest 2–3 specific actions based on what you see. Explain any metric that is outside a healthy range.
07 GA4
Traffic Source Analysis
Analyze the following GA4 traffic source breakdown for our site over [date range]: [paste: organic search, direct, referral, paid, social, email session and user data]. Tell me which channels are driving quality, engaged traffic vs. just raw volume. Where is there overdependence or a clear missed opportunity? Recommend one specific action per underperforming channel.
08 GA4
Conversion Funnel Diagnosis
Here is our GA4 funnel data for [goal: form submission / newsletter signup / contact page visit / sponsored content click-through]: [paste step-by-step drop-off data]. Identify where the biggest drop-off is happening and give me 3 hypotheses for why. For each hypothesis, suggest a specific fix or test I can run to validate it — including any GA4 event or goal tracking adjustments I should make.
09 GA4
Monthly Analytics Summary
Using our GA4 data from [month] — including Engagement Rate, Sessions, Total Users, Views, Pages Per Session, and % of Returning Visitors: [paste metrics] — write a concise monthly analytics summary I can share with a non-technical stakeholder or advertiser. No jargon. Lead with the most important takeaway, contextualize the numbers against last month or last year, and close with what we're focusing on next month.
10 GA4
Returning vs. New Visitor Analysis
Our GA4 data shows a Returning Visitor rate of [X%] against a New Visitor rate of [Y%] for [date range]. Total Users: [N]. Interpret what this split tells us about audience loyalty and content performance. Is this ratio healthy for a [regional lifestyle / bridal / media] publication? What content or CTA strategies could improve return visit rates?

Sponsored Content & Sales Materials

5 prompts
11 Sales
Media Kit / One-Sheet Copy
Write the copy for a digital media kit one-sheet for [publication/brand] targeting [advertiser type: luxury / wedding / lifestyle / regional]. Include: a value proposition headline, audience snapshot with key stats, reach and engagement metrics, advertising opportunities overview, and a closing CTA. Tone: confident and data-forward without feeling like a hard sell. Keep it to one tight page of copy.
12 Sales
Sponsored Content Proposal
Write a sponsorship proposal for [publication] pitching [campaign or content package] to [advertiser]. Structure it as: the opportunity, the audience fit, the deliverables, the projected impact, and investment. Frame everything from the advertiser's perspective — what they gain — not just what we're offering. Include a placeholder for our GA4 reach metrics. Keep it under one page.
13 Sponsored
Sponsored Content Article
Write a sponsored content article for [publication] on behalf of [brand partner] about [topic]. It should read as editorial — genuinely useful and engaging — not as an advertisement. Naturally integrate the brand's key message and include a soft CTA near the end. Reader: [audience profile]. Word count: [400–600 / 600–900]. The article must be optimized for SEO, AI search (Google SGE, ChatGPT, Perplexity), GEO (Generative Engine Optimization), and AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — especially in the first paragraph, which should directly answer the reader's likely search intent using natural language, target keywords, and a clear, quotable statement that AI engines can surface as a featured answer.
14 Sponsored
Campaign Recap for Advertiser
Write a post-campaign recap report for [advertiser] summarizing the results of [campaign name]. Include: campaign overview, key metrics delivered vs. promised (use our GA4 data: [paste relevant stats]), highlights such as top-performing placements or content moments, and a forward-looking recommendation for the next campaign. Tone: confident and client-ready. Lead with the win.
15 Sales
Advertiser Follow-Up Email
Write a follow-up email to [advertiser / prospect] after [a proposal send / a campaign wrap / an initial conversation]. The goal is to [re-engage / upsell / renew / get a decision]. Keep it under 100 words, reference something specific from our last interaction, and make the ask clear without being pushy. Include a subject line.

Metrics & Data Storytelling

4 prompts
16 Storytelling
Turn Data into a Narrative
Here are performance metrics from [campaign / month / channel]: [paste data]. Don't just summarize the numbers — build a story around them. What happened, why it matters, what it means for the business, and what comes next. Write it so a non-marketer — an advertiser, publisher, or executive — would find it both clear and compelling in a meeting setting.
17 Storytelling
Explain a Metric to a Client
Explain what [metric: Engagement Rate / Pages Per Session / % Returning Visitors / Click-Through Rate / Open Rate / Bounce Rate] means in plain language for an advertiser who is not a marketer. Cover: what it measures, what a healthy number looks like for a regional media brand, and why they should care. Keep it under 150 words with no jargon.
18 Reporting
Quarterly & Monthly Performance Report
Using this data from [month / quarter]: [paste GA4 metrics + HubSpot email metrics + any campaign data], write a performance report for [internal team / advertiser / leadership]. Structure: 3-sentence executive summary, channel-by-channel highlights (website, email, sponsored), what underperformed and why, and 3 priorities for the next period. Written in human language — data-informed, not data-drowning.
19 Reporting
Benchmark Comparison Write-Up
Here are our current metrics: [paste GA4 and/or HubSpot data]. Apply industry benchmarks for a [regional media / lifestyle publication / bridal media] brand and write a comparison narrative. Where are we outperforming? Where are we lagging? Frame every gap as an opportunity rather than a failure, and suggest one specific action for each area where we're behind.
